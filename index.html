About

The longer
version

I came to systematic investing the same way most serious people do — through frustration with the alternative.

DALBAR has tracked retail investor behavior for 30 years. The finding is consistent: individual investors underperform their own funds by 1–4% annually. Not from bad picks. From behavioral exits — selling at the bottom of a drawdown they hadn't planned for.

The market is noisy by design. Most of what gets called "analysis" is pattern-matching dressed up as insight. Loud, confident, and frequently wrong.

I started building my own tools.

Momentum Strategies
Hidden Markov Models
Linear Regression Slope — TQQQ / NASDAQ Futures

Python became the language I used to test whether an idea actually held up — or just sounded good. What emerged from that process wasn't just code. It was a way of framing every investment decision across four dimensions. I call it 4D Investing.

The book is in progress. The YouTube channel is where the thinking happens in real time.

I'm not a fund manager. I'm not selling alpha. I'm a practitioner-learner who believes that the HOW of investing — the mental model, the systematic discipline, the willingness to sit with uncertainty — is the only durable edge most individual investors will ever have.

The strategy works. Until the regime changes. — LRS on TQQQ (2022–2024)
